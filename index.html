Cabadaich = Chat

Chat in Gaelic with Gaels around the world and down the road. From beginner to advanced, youth to elders, we are all part of one Gàidhealtachd. Cabadaich is your official passport to the global Gaelic community.

Share Learning Resources

Beginners, you'll find help screens and dictionary links to help you participate. Advanced learners and teachers, you'll find great conversations, sources for interesting materials, and much more. Young Gaels, there are special sections for your interests and conversations too.

Find Collaborators

Imagine finding another Gael who wants to make a film about Gaelic zombies or write new Gaelic songs about climate change or who wants to start a Gaelic wedding planning service. Cabadaich helps you find the team you need to work and live and play in Gaelic.


Polish Your Dialect

Maybe you heard your Great-Uncle or your Grandmother use a particular phrase all the time and you aren't quite sure what it means. Bammoch or Bonnoch? We're pretty sure someone in the global community of Gaels would have pretty good information to help you figure it out.

Avoid Facebook

Some of us have endured years of cat videos and sappy Facebook memes in order to stay connected with our fellow Gaels, but finding Gaelic on FB is akin to searching for the needle in a haystack. Cabadaich will be all Gaelic, except one special "I need to ask in English" channel. So even if you see a bunch of cat pictures, the discussion will all be in Gaelic, and really, what harm is there in a few cat pictures?.

Locate a Gael

Imagine a "Gael Locator" that beeps when you come within 50 feet of another Gaelic speaker. Okay, well we're not wuite there yet with Cabadaich, but if you were traveling to Ottawa or Raleigh or Frankfurt or Waipu, you could post on Cabadaich and ask if there are any Gaelic speakers living where you are traveling and maybe you could meet for a drink and a ceilidh.

Be a Part of Building Gaelic Nation

  • Contribute Money

    Passion, determination, and courage can bring a lot of ideas to life, but there comes a time when every company needs cold hard cash. Money helps speed up the ability to hire people, advertise, and create excellent quality content and features to serve customers' needs. Maybe you love the idea of creating Gaelic apps, new Gaelic tools, and Gaelic business that support Gaelic jobs. If so, contact us, and we can have a conversation about how you can donate. We'll be starting a funding campaign soon, and it would be great to have a few champions lined up early. Every $5 and $10 and $20 helps. We'll offer some cool thank yous to our early champions.

  • Give Time & Knowledge

    Be a volunteer on our testing teams or maybe you have some coding and IT skills that you would like to share to build and support new features for Cabadaich and upcoming Gaelic apps. Reach out, we'll try to find ways to bring all our skills together in beautiful ways.

  • Invite Your Gaelic Friends

    Cabadaich is a place to connect with Gaels - those you know and those you have yet to meet. By inviting your friends to join you in Cabadaich, you can help the Cabadaich community grow and that helps everyone.
